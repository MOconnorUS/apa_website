Integrated Logistics Support (ILS)/Acquisition Logistics

Our Integrated Logistics Support (ILS)/Acquisition Logistics services ensure that your operations run smoothly and efficiently by leveraging our PTO framework and encompassing all 12 ILS elements. As logisticians, we understand the critical role logistics plays in the lifecycle of systems and equipment from initial acquisition through sustainment.

  • Personnel Adoption: We provide training programs, onboarding support, and continuous professional development for your logistics personnel, ensuring they are well-equipped to manage the complexities of acquisition logistics. Training includes understanding and adhering to established change policies and effectively participating in change control boards to ensure structured and compliant transitions.

  • Technology Adoption: While we focus on the people aspect, we also identify ways that tools and technology can be leveraged to optimize performance and achieve better outcomes in acquisition logistics. This includes adhering to established change policies and effectively participating in change control boards. Understanding the technical requirements and constraints of each change is critical to ensuring that technology adoption aligns with organizational goals and enhances overall performance.

  • Organizational Adoption: We optimize your logistics processes and create new ones as necessary to manage change effectively, helping your organization adapt to new acquisition systems and practices smoothly. We manage change processes through structure change policies and change control boards, ensuring seamless transitions, maintaining operational continuity, and enhancing overall performance.

The 12 ILS Elements

  1. Product Support Management: Overseeing all logistics support activities throughout the lifecycle of the product.

  2. Design Interface: Ensuring logistics considerations are integrated into system design.

  3. Sustaining Engineering: Overseeing all logistics support activities throughout the lifecycle of the product.

  4. Supply Support: Managing the supply chain to ensure the availability of necessary materials and components.

  5. Maintenance Planning and Management: Developing and implementing maintenance strategies to sustain system readiness.

  6. Packaging, Handling, Storage, and Transportation (PHS&T): Ensuring safe and efficient handling and transport of materials.

  7. Technical Data: Managing technical documentation and data throughout the system lifecycle.

  8. Support Equipment: Ensuring the availability and maintenance of equipment needed to support system operations.

  9. Training and Training Support: Providing training programs to ensure personnel can support the system.

  10. Manpower and Personnel: Planning and managing the workforce needed to support the system.

  11. Facilities and Infrastructure: Developing and maintaining the facilities necessary to support the system.

  12. Computer Resources: Ensuring the availability and functionality of computer resources needed to support system operations.
